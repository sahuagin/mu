# Licensing philosophy

`mu` is source-available under a delayed-open license. The current license is Business Source License 1.1 with an Additional Use Grant for personal,
educational, research, noncommercial, internal-evaluation, and open-source-project use. On the change date, this version converts to BSD-3-Clause.

This is not an OSI-approved open-source license today. That is intentional.

## Why not plain BSD-3-Clause today?

BSD-3-Clause is the license tradition this project wants to end up in. It is simple, permissive, and carries the non-endorsement clause that matters here:
derived products may not use the author's name to promote themselves without permission.

But `mu` is also a fresh distillation of hard-earned systems and agent-runtime experience. Individuals using, studying, and extending `mu` are not the same thing
as corporations absorbing fresh architectural leverage into proprietary products.

`mu` is intended to become part of the commons over time, but not as a zero-cost transfer of hard-won systems experience to entities whose incentives are
extraction-first.

## What is allowed without a commercial license?

The license is meant to allow broad noncommercial and community use:

- individuals using `mu` for themselves;
- educational use;
- academic, public-interest, or noncommercial research;
- internal evaluation, testing, benchmarking, and proof-of-concept work;
- use in, and contribution to, open-source projects under OSI-approved licenses, subject to the limits in `LICENSE`.

If you are a person hacking on agents, studying the design, or adapting ideas in an open project, the intent is that you can do that.

## What requires a commercial license?

Commercial production use requires a separate license. In particular, the license is meant to reserve commercial rights for:

- paid products, hosted services, agent platforms, developer tools, or consulting deliverables based on `mu`;
- proprietary infrastructure that incorporates `mu` or derivatives of it;
- model-training, fine-tuning, evaluation, benchmarking, ranking, retrieval-indexing, embedding, or automated-ingestion pipelines;
- use by or on behalf of companies whose primary business is LLMs, automated coding agents, agent platforms, or model-training infrastructure.

The short version: if a company wants to turn `mu` into commercial leverage without sharing that leverage back, that is a commercial transaction.

## Why delayed-open?

The delayed-open model is the compromise:

- the source is visible now;
- people can learn from it now;
- noncommercial/community use is allowed now;
- commercial capture is reserved while the ideas are fresh;
- this version eventually converts to BSD-3-Clause.

That honors the BSD instinct without pretending that corporations and individual hackers have the same incentives.
