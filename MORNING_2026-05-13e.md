## Loop-guard / Read-before-Edit observations

- **Loop-guard never tripped.** No identical-tool-call sequences of length ≥4 occurred. The threshold of 4 was conservative for this experiment but is the right floor (experiment 4 spun 11+ — would have been caught at ~36% of the cap).
- **Read-before-Edit instruction worked perfectly.** Every `Edit` call followed a `Read` of the same file. Zero `File has not been read yet` errors observed. The explicit prompt instruction (plus the `tool-conventions.md` doc update) closed out the experiment-4 failure mode.
- **One unexpected mini-loop avoided**: when `br close mu-1n3` failed due to reversed dep edges, the worker diagnosed the error from the error text (`blocked by: mu-ovl`) and pivoted to `--force` rather than retrying the same command. ~$0.08 spent diagnosing; net behavior matches the protocol's spirit (diagnose, don't retry blindly).
